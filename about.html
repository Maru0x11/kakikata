About

Who I am, why this blog exists, and what's coming next

Hey, I'm Maru

Sophomore CS student. Fedora Linux user. Guitar player. Someone who finds Japanese culture genuinely beautiful.

I started this blog for two reasons:

  1. Learn JavaScript — by building something real, not following tutorials
  2. Share the messy journey — because 80% of new programmers feel stuck, and nobody talks about it

Why "Kakikata"?

書き方 (kakikata) means "a way of writing" in Japanese. The name was an accident — but it stuck. This blog is my way of writing about programming, music, and whatever else I'm learning.

What's Coming

  • A static site generator — built from scratch
  • More posts about JavaScript, C, and Linux
  • Music and guitar-related writing
  • Open source everything
