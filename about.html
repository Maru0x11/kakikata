Hey, I'm Maru

Sophomore CS student. Fedora Linux user. Guitar player. Passionate about learning Languages and getting to know more about different cultures, I find the Japanese culture and the German one as well genuinely beautiful... more on that later though.

I started this blog for two reasons:

  1. Learn JavaScript though one could really argue if thats even possible... but more on that later
  2. Share the messy journey because people always share the highlights but nobody talks about the suck, which is basically 80% of the entire journey - so yeah if you were looking for another trust-me-bro-these-tips-will-help-you-become-a-better-programmer kinda blog - I'm sorry pal you're on the wrong blog - I wanted to talk about the suck - cause maybe I might be able to help somebody, he / she feeling less alone.

Why "Kakikata"?

書き方 (kakikata) means "a way of writing" in Japanese. The name, I don't even know where'd it come from, I just found it cool and I wanted to have japanese involved in the whole thing somehow
